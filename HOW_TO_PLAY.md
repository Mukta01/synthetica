# 🧬 Synthetica: How to Play

Welcome to Synthetica! You have just been hired as a Lead Genetic Engineer. Your job is to design custom proteins that can survive in extreme alien environments. 

Don't know anything about genetics? Don't worry! This guide will teach you everything you need to know.

---

## 🔬 1. The Basics of DNA
DNA is the instruction manual for building proteins. It is made up of four basic building blocks (bases), represented by letters:
- **A** (Adenine)
- **C** (Cytosine)
- **G** (Guanine)
- **T** (Thymine)

### Codons (The 3-Letter Words)
The cellular machinery reads these letters in **groups of three**, called a **Codon**. 
For example, the sequence `ATGGCATAG` is read as three codons: `ATG`, `GCA`, and `TAG`.

### Amino Acids (The Protein Blocks)
Every codon translates into exactly one **Amino Acid**. Amino acids string together to form a protein.
For example, the codon `GCA` translates into the amino acid *Alanine*. 

---

## 🧪 2. Protein Properties
Different amino acids have different physical properties. In Synthetica, we care about three main survival traits:

1. 🔥 **Heat Stability:** Can the protein survive in extreme heat (like a volcano)?
2. 💧 **Hydrophobicity:** Does the protein repel water (useful in deep-sea environments)?
3. ☠️ **Toxin Resistance:** Can the protein survive in acidic or toxic pools?

*Tip: You don't need to memorize these! Use the **Genome Codex** button in the game to look up which codons create which properties.*

---

## 🎮 3. How to Play a Level

When you enter a lab environment, you will be given an **Objective**. 

For example: *"Create a protein that is at least 50% Heat Stable to survive the Magma Vents."*

### Step 1: Analyze the DNA
You will be given a starting DNA sequence. Look at the **Protein Preview** panel to see what amino acids are currently being produced and what their properties are.

### Step 2: Mutate the DNA
Your goal is to change the DNA so it produces the traits you need. You have three tools:
- **Swap:** Replace one letter with another (e.g., change an `A` to a `G`). This changes the codon, which changes the amino acid!
- **Insert:** Add a brand new letter. *(Warning: This shifts all the following letters over, completely changing how the rest of the codons are read!)*
- **Delete:** Remove a letter. *(Warning: This also shifts all following letters!)*

### Step 3: Consult the Codex
Need more Heat Stability? Open the **Genome Codex**. Search for "Heat Stable" to find which codons produce heat-stable amino acids, then use your mutation tools to type those exact 3-letter codes into your DNA sequence.

### Step 4: Compile & Test
Once your Protein Preview shows that you meet the environmental requirements (e.g., 50% Heat Stability), hit the **Compile** button! The system will simulate injecting your protein into the alien environment. 

---

## 🏆 4. Scoring and Leaderboards

Anyone can brute-force a solution, but a *master* genetic engineer does it efficiently. 

Your score is calculated based on two factors:
1. **Fewer Mutations:** Every time you use a Swap, Insert, or Delete, it costs you points. Try to achieve the objective with the absolute minimum number of changes.
2. **Speed:** The faster you solve the puzzle, the higher your score.

Scores are ranked on the **Global Leaderboard**. Get 700+ points to earn a 3-Star rating!

---

### 💡 Quick Tips for Beginners
- Always check the **Codex** before mutating.
- **Swapping** is usually safer than Inserting or Deleting, because it only affects one codon.
- Inserting or Deleting causes a **"Frameshift"**. Because DNA is read in groups of 3, inserting a letter shifts the entire reading frame, changing *every single amino acid* after the insertion. Use this to your advantage to completely rewrite a sequence with just one mutation!
